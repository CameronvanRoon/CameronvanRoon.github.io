Project Idea

My idea for a project is a Facebook app that functions as a multiplayer AR videogame. The app makes posts such as fake news stories and fictional profile posts and fictional posts by the users to the players’ Facebook feeds in order to create a fictional work and allows interactions between players and the fabricated world and between each other.
Each of the players is given their own specific goals, roles and abilities. There would be an apparent universal goal involving a fabricated real-world event (likely a supernatural phenomenon such as a zombie outbreak, alien invasion, or other supernatural creatures on the loose). However, most players also have their own individual goals, which may come into conflict with one another. It would require subterfuge and the ability to solve mysteries.

Motivation

There are 2.32 Billion active Facebook users world wide. People spend hours upon hours each week looking at their feeds, which are generally filled with mindless memes and uninteresting content. My idea is to spice up users’ social media experience by creating an engaging and fun fictional world they can interact with while they browse. It’s a fun game that can help bring friends together and enrich their experience together whether they’re neighbours or oceans apart by bringing the joys of games like Mafia or Werewolves to them with little effort.

Description

Players would sign up to the app which would then need to create or join an instance of the game. The host could manage who is involved in the instance and what type/duration of the game the party would be engaging in. Once the party has been designated, the app begins to make targeted Facebook posts to the players. Both through these posts and/or through options within the app interface, the players would be granted a number of daily actions as determined by the events in the game and their roles/abilities.
Effectively, the project creates a session of a game similar to Mafia or Werewolves that blends almost seamlessly into their social media platforms and allows for a fun new way to interact with its content. It allows for a group of players to interact with the game as much or as little as they like and potentially over a longer period of time.
Major events in the story (as conveyed through Facebook posts) would be shaped by the actions of the players as they try to avoid communal catastrophe and/or accomplish their own goals.
The game could also fake posts from the players themselves, including life events such as employment changes or even death (only visible to the other players, who have opted into the session and marked as being part of the game).
Sessions could take place over any of several different timeframes, from a more conventional single-night session over a few hours to a week or a month, or potentially even longer, which would be a unique and driving feature of the game.
Players would be able to utilize Facebook’s own in-built messenger functions to discuss aspects of the game. The idea is that the interactions of the game could spill over into the other social media interactions of the players.

Features

Game Settings

Players can determine the number of participants, the game duration, and eventually even different themes, such as vampires, witches, mafia, aliens, Cthulhu, etc.

Player Roles

Players are assigned unique roles with different goals and abilities, such as citizen, alien body-snatcher, sheriff, reporter, psychic, cult fanatic, doctor, scientist, FBI/Illuminati agent, or town mayor.

Non-Player Characters

Each with limited interaction means, but who are important for story events.

Major Events

Players will learn about and interact with major story events, such as a nearby UFO sighting (by a player or third-party), a UFO crash, an alien abduction or the discovery of a secret Area-51-esque complex.

Contextual Event Interactions

Players are given the opportunity to respond to major events in ways such as going to a location to investigate, interviewing witnesses, researching the history of the area or ignoring it. Contextual interaction could also include role-specific actions like trying to intimidate witnesses, posting contradictory or fake reports or attempting to destroy evidence.

Daily/Weekly Actions

Players also have the option of using daily or weekly actions. These could include calling a community trial to determine whether an individual is secretly an alien body-snatcher. They could also use daily or weekly abilities unique to their role, such as a sheriff or reporter investigating, a body-snatched player attempting to take control of a player or NPC.

Tools and Technology

The simplicity of the idea means it could mostly be accomplished through the use of Facebook’s built-in app development tools (as available through https://developers.facebook.com/). The app may also require some extra basic programming. Depending on the way it is approached, one of the more challenging aspects of the process would be in the creation of art assets. This could be achieved through a variety of technologies, such as Adobe Photoshop and Final Cut Pro or Lightworks.

Skills Required

The project will require a high amount of familiarity with the Facebook app development tools. It will also require some basic programming understanding, likely using java, and an ability to create or ascertain the art assets necessary to help bring the user an immersive experience. The development of art assets could require an intermediate to advanced understanding of software such as adobe photoshop, and potentially video editing software such as Final Cut Pro or Lightworks, if video is to be used.

Outcome

If this task is completed, there will be a new, unique and fun game for people to play on Facebook. It will spice up users’ social media experiences and bring players closer together. It will be a solid foundation for expansion and refinement of the games core mechanics. Developing this game will also give the developers useful experience in app development, social media integration, creating user interfaces, and game development skills. It will also give us experience developing software in a team and the opportunity to interact with an ongoing development cycle.
